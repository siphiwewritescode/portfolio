Concept

Neuro Memory Clinic wants to improve its understanding of residentsâ€™ health and therapy participation. They aim to track medication in-take, therapy visits, and the conditions affecting residents to identify areas that need attention and ensure better care for everyone.

Implementation

Data Preparation:

I worked with Excel data that included details about residents (like age, gender, and conditions), medications (prescribed and missed), and therapy visits (behavioral and physical). I ensured the data was clean and organised by removing duplicates and blank records.

Designing the Report:

The report includes two cards showing total residents and average age, three slicers (Gender, Age Group, Type of Condition), and visuals such as a clustered column chart (residents by type of condition and gender), a stacked column chart (prescribed vs. missed medications by month), and a line chart (therapy visits by month).

Insights Discovered

Medication In-take:

Certain months had higher missed medications, which might need further investigation to find out why (e.g., staffing issues, medication availability, or resident health changes).

Conditions by Gender:

Some conditions were more common among men or women. For instance, women might have a higher percentage of certain chronic conditions, which could guide treatment planning.

Therapy Visits:

Behavioral therapy visits were more frequent than physical therapy in some months, suggesting a need to balance or evaluate the effectiveness of therapy programs at the clinic.

Resident Overview:

The average age and the distribution of residents by gender and condition helped identify the primary demographic being served, ensuring programs are tailored to their needs.
